Visual Alignment

Joji's Website

https://jojimusic.com/#tour

Joji's website is a spectacular example of Visual Alignment. What it does best is actually very simple: everything is centered. It's simplistic design conveys the idea that there's not too much more you need to know. From the screenshot you can see album art, which is actually a .gif, and you can see a tour date. Outside of the screenshot there's more tour dates and there's a link to his merchandise. That's it. It's not overwhelming, it's not in your face, it's actually the opposite. It's calming. It's a lot like his music actually, which I would note is really great design.

Color

Surfaces Website

https://www.surfacesmusic.com/

The Surfaces website is an exemplar of color choice. One look and you can tell that they had a clear color palette in mind. In the background you can see several shades of purple and pink that blend together into a sunset, these shades are contrasted by nothing less than the color of the sun. The mountain in the back is a hint of bluegray that goes very well with the lighter shades of purple higher up in the sky. The green on the tree matches the bush and the guitarist's shirt very well as well as the water beneath their feet. The hair of the two artists actually matches the sun and their pants the sky. On top of that they have a very interactive design with menu items sliding in as they walk the beach.

Rule of 2/3s

Nike's website

https://www.nike.com/

The Nike Website is a great example of following the two thirds rule, and we can see it right off the bat with the first image. The first out of the two images is taking up approximately 2/3 of the screen, with the focal points being on the runners. The text follows this rule as well with the bigger text being the one-liner "New moves, best you".
